# Public Naming And Fan-art Risk

Checked on 2026-07-19. This is a practical release assessment, not legal
advice, a copyright opinion, or a trademark-clearance opinion.

## Current Publication State

The repository is public at
[FrameCoreWorks/naruto-codex-deliberation-council](https://github.com/FrameCoreWorks/naruto-codex-deliberation-council),
and `v1.0.0` has a public
[GitHub Release](https://github.com/FrameCoreWorks/naruto-codex-deliberation-council/releases/tag/v1.0.0).
Publication occurred without documented franchise-rights clearance. Technical
validation and the MIT license for original repository material do not change
that status.

The repository name is deliberately franchise-derived; it is not neutral. The
exact `$naruto` trigger and character-derived process labels are memorable
compatibility names, but public functional use is not automatically risk-free.

## Assessment

- Naruto is an active, commercially licensed entertainment franchise. Public
  use of its name, characters, or recognizable visual identity can create
  copyright, trademark, affiliation, and endorsement concerns.
- The banner at
  `assets/naruto-codex-deliberation-council-banner.png` intentionally depicts
  recognizable Naruto, Kakashi, Yamato, and Tsunade through franchise-derived
  hairstyles, clothing, headbands, facial markings, names, and Hokage framing.
  This exact-character route has materially more rights risk than original,
  non-franchise artwork.
- Newly generated or independently drawn fan art can still contain protected
  character expression. The absence of an official logo, copied panel, scan,
  or dialogue does not by itself establish permission to distribute it.
- An unofficial, no-affiliation disclaimer helps state project provenance, but
  it does not grant copyright, trademark, merchandising, publicity, or other
  rights.
- The user-selected banner joins `CODEX` and `DELIBERATION` tightly. Recording
  that known visual deviation is accurate QA metadata, not franchise approval
  or title clearance.
- No complete USPTO, WIPO, copyright, publicity-rights, or country-by-country
  clearance review has been performed.

## Mitigations Already Applied

- `NOTICE.md` identifies the project and banner as unofficial and unaffiliated.
- The banner is isolated at one documented asset path and expressly excluded
  from the MIT license.
- The repository includes no official logo, manga scan, copied panel, manga
  dialogue, or official brand asset.
- Asset provenance, digest, publication state, user selection, known title
  deviation, and `rights_clearance: not_cleared` are recorded in
  `manifest/assets.json`.
- Tsunade Senju is the public identity of the parent Hokage role only. There is
  no seventh runtime profile or additional solver.
- The fourth Naruto instance is defined by the Empirical Verifier method rather
  than represented as another fictional character.

These measures improve factual disclosure. They do not amount to rights
clearance.

## Available Risk Decisions

The publisher should document one of these routes:

1. Obtain appropriate rights-holder permission or qualified legal review for
   the exact public use of the banner and franchise-derived labels.
2. Replace the banner with fully original, non-franchise artwork and consider
   neutralizing public role, repository, and trigger names while preserving the
   technical deliberation design.
3. Keep the current public fan-art route while knowingly accepting unresolved
   rights risk. The MIT license and disclaimer do not transfer that risk or
   provide the missing permission.

A technical `1.0.1` validation pass is not a legal-clearance result. If names
or artwork change, update the README, notice, manifests, checksums, fixtures,
profile labels, acceptance tests, repository metadata, and release materials
together. Do not rename only one layer of the protocol.
